Invoice Week #1

  

Date & Time: 6.13.2026, 12:18 PM

Less than a month ago, I was accepted into the SPARK A2 program at the Digital Clinic. It was an exciting moment, especially after spending so much time applying for jobs. During my interview, I shared that I was looking for a position where I could gain hands-on experience in graphic design and UX/UI. I also expressed my desire to use my skills to create meaningful solutions that help people.

When the SPARK A2 team contacted me to tell me I had been accepted, I felt excited and valued. I was even happier when they matched me with the startup-company and gave me the opportunity to work with the CEO/founder as my mentor. The startup-company is dedicated to improving the K–12 Individualized Education Program (IEP) process.

During the onboarding week, I learned about the IEP system and the challenges surrounding it. I was surprised to discover the significant gap between what parents need to know and what they actually know, and how that gap can directly affect a child's educational experience. I also learned that educators and school systems face their own challenges in ensuring that every child receives a truly personalized IEP.

Crystal and I were paired with the startup-company team, and we are both motivated to contribute to the company's mission. It appears that Crystal will focus on research and analysis, while I will concentrate on design. Together, our different responsibilities will help create a better user experience for families and educators. We have already begun collaborating effectively, and I look forward to seeing how our teamwork develops.

My first project focused on creating marketing content. I refreshed some of the startup-company's existing materials and created new short-form videos, including branded intro and outro animations featuring the startup-company logo. It felt rewarding to make a meaningful contribution so early in the internship.

Next week, I plan to produce a complete educational video about the IEP process. If the workflow goes smoothly, I hope to create additional educational videos more efficiently. I also plan to continue my marketing training and strengthen my skills in digital communication.

As I reflect on these first weeks, I feel grateful for this opportunity. It aligns with my goal of using design to help others, while giving me the chance to grow professionally through real-world experience. I am excited to see where this journey will lead.

Invoice Week #3-5

  

Date & Time: 7.8.2026, 15:16 PM

It has been a while since I last wrote here. The past two weeks have been incredibly productive, filled with meaningful work and valuable lessons. My mentor at the startup-company, the CEO/founder, has been pleased with my work and continues to challenge me to develop new skills while encouraging me to step outside my comfort zone.

More importantly, I realized that learning new skills is only part of the journey. I also learned how to become a better learner. Although I have always considered myself a good listener, I discovered that I need to put even more effort into truly listening, understanding feedback, and applying it.

One accomplishment I feel especially proud of is completing the "Special Needs Awareness" campaign. The campaign provides clear, educational insights into the 13 disability categories recognized in special education. As part of this project, I created 16 educational videos explaining each disability within the IEP process.

Along the way, I learned an important marketing lesson. A successful campaign is not just about creating one strong piece of content—it is about reinforcing the core message from different angles. To help the audience better understand and retain the information, I planned an additional six to seven supporting posts for each disability. In total, I created 94 assets for this campaign.

I also produced 26 videos celebrating American and cultural holidays. I made a conscious effort to be inclusive by recognizing a diverse range of holidays and communities. This project taught me another valuable marketing lesson: holiday content should pair authentic holiday greetings with visuals that prominently feature the holiday itself while still reflecting the brand's identity. That balance helps capture attention while maintaining brand consistency.

Looking back, these past two weeks were about much more than producing content. They reminded me that growth comes from staying curious, listening carefully, embracing feedback, and continually pushing beyond what feels comfortable.

Thanks for reading.

  

Invoice Week #6

  

Date & Time: 7.15.2026, 05:28 PM

This week has been intensive with training and research. I focused on finding the best movie-making program to use while considering the remaining time available for the internship. I needed to evaluate factors such as credit availability, pricing, and access to free services.

I explored Veo3 by Google and researched its capabilities. The program can create high-quality text-to-video and text-to-image content. Although I was impressed by the quality of the results, I decided not to use it because of the cost. I will consider using it for future projects when the budget allows.

For now, I chose to use Lumen5 for several reasons:

  • Lumen5 can transform written content into videos.
  • It provides a built-in library of templates and creative resources.
  • I can upload images and audio files.
  • I can use prompts to generate specific video concepts.

Advantages: Lumen5 offers flexible options, including templates, custom designs, and different frame measurements. It includes libraries for music and visual styles. Users can generate videos through prompts or customize each slide individually. Another advantage is that it offers a limited free plan.

Disadvantages: The final results can sometimes feel generic. The Pro version is relatively expensive, and editing capabilities are limited in the free version. For example, I found it difficult to replace elements between slides, move an image from one slide to another, or place audio on top of a specific layer.
